# Generative AI Workflows for Creating Accessible Educational Content in Higher Education: A Scoping Review

This repository contains the supplementary materials, datasets, and analysis resources supporting the scoping review entitled:

**“Generative AI Workflows for Creating Accessible Educational Content in Higher Education: A Scoping Review”**

The study systematically examines the technical integration of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) to support the creation, adaptation, and personalization of accessible educational content in higher education, structured across four analytical dimensions: **knowledge modeling, generative architectures, post-processing, and evaluation**.

---

## Repository Structure

The repository is organized as follows:

### **Dataset-GenIA-UDL**
Contains the integrated technical matrix and processed data extracted from the 71 included studies.

- **Bibliographic Data:** Full metadata of the analyzed corpus (2022–2026)  
- **Research Questions Tables:** Structured tables mapping the selected studies to each research question (RQs)  
- **Synthesis Tables:** Consolidated tables summarizing key findings, technical approaches, and relevant patterns identified across the literature  

### **PRISMA-ScR-Checklist**
The completed PRISMA-ScR checklist, ensuring transparency and methodological rigor according to the 20 essential items for scoping reviews.

---

## Methodology

This review followed the **PRISMA-ScR guidelines** to map the intersection of GenAI and inclusive higher education.

- **Databases Searched:** IEEE Xplore, Scopus, Web of Science, ERIC, and ACM Digital Library  
- **Timeframe:** January 2022 – February 2026  
- **Selection:** Out of 442 initial records, 71 studies met the rigorous inclusion criteria focused on technical implementation and educational accessibility  

---

## Highlights

- PRISMA-ScR-based mapping of GenAI workflows for inclusive higher education  
- Taxonomy of workflows across knowledge modeling, architectures, and evaluation  
- Identification of critical gaps in accessibility integration across AI pipelines  
- Evaluation remains heterogeneous, with a shift toward hybrid multi-dimensional metrics  
- Proposed architectural pipeline for controllable and accessible AI generation
